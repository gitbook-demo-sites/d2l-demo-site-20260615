# Support routes and ownership

One of the strongest signals in the public Brightspace help content is that account access belongs to the customer's institution. This page makes that operating model explicit.

## Ownership model

<table>
  <thead>
    <tr>
      <th>Question</th>
      <th>Primary owner</th>
      <th>Best destination</th>
    </tr>
  </thead>
  <tbody>
    <tr>
      <td>Where do I log in?</td>
      <td>Institution plus public docs</td>
      <td>Help-center login page and login finder</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>I forgot my password</td>
      <td>Institution support team</td>
      <td>IT support or Brightspace administrator</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>I want release notifications</td>
      <td>D2L documentation team</td>
      <td>Release-note subscription page</td>
    </tr>
    <tr>
      <td>I need integration details for data exports</td>
      <td>Developer platform team</td>
      <td>Data sets API Reference</td>
    </tr>
  </tbody>
</table>

## Recommended support language

{% hint style="warning" %}
Your organization manages your Brightspace account and login credentials. If you cannot sign in after following the documented steps, contact your institution's IT support team or Brightspace administrator.
{% endhint %}

## Why keep this page

- It reduces ambiguity across learner, admin, and support flows
- It gives customer teams one URL to share for access ownership questions
- It keeps the API section focused on developers rather than account triage
