# Logging in to Brightspace

If you cannot sign in, the first goal is to identify *which login path your organization expects*. Brightspace is deployed by schools and organizations with different account models, so the right answer depends on the institution that gave you access.

{% hint style="danger" %}
D2L does not normally reset your password, recover your username, or create your account. Your school or organization's support team owns those actions.
{% endhint %}

## Start here

{% stepper %}
{% step %}
### Find the correct login page

Your organization may send you to:

- a school or company portal
- a Microsoft or Google sign-in page
- a custom Brightspace login page

If you do not know which one applies, use the [Brightspace Login Finder](https://login-finder.d2l.com/).
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Identify the login method

Most users see one of three patterns:

- *Single sign-on (SSO)* through the institution's identity provider
- *Local login* with a Brightspace username and password
- *Two-factor authentication* layered on top of local login
{% endstep %}

{% step %}
### Escalate to the right owner

If the page is correct but access still fails, contact your institution's IT support or Brightspace administrator for password resets, account recovery, and access confirmation.
{% endstep %}
{% endstepper %}

## Common issues

<details>
<summary>I do not know where to log in</summary>

Use the [Login Finder](https://login-finder.d2l.com/) first. If it still is not obvious, ask your institution for the official sign-in link instead of guessing between multiple portals.
</details>

<details>
<summary>I forgot my password</summary>

If your organization uses SSO, reset the password in Microsoft, Google, or the institution-managed identity provider. If it uses local login, ask your Brightspace administrator to reset it.
</details>

<details>
<summary>My login is not working</summary>

Confirm that you have the right page, the right username format, and the right sign-in method. If all three are correct, ask the institution to verify that your account is active and allowed into the course or tenant you expect.
</details>

<details>
<summary>I need help with two-factor authentication</summary>

Two-factor setup is controlled by the institution's Brightspace or identity configuration. Support teams should provide the recovery path for lost devices, rotated email addresses, or authenticator resets.
</details>
